Hospitality Furniture for Hotels, Restaurants & Bars

Contract-grade furniture for high-traffic hospitality spaces — built for the wear, aligned with your brand, delivered on schedule.

Overview

Furniture That Works the Shift as Hard as You Do

Hospitality furniture has a harder life than almost anything else in commercial interiors. It gets sat on, leaned on, spilled on and dragged across the floor, every day, by strangers. It has to meet UK fire-safety regulations — typically Crib 5 for upholstered items — without looking like a compromise. And it has to do all of that while carrying your brand: the thing that makes a guest post a photo, tell a friend, come back.

We supply contract-grade hospitality furniture for hotels, restaurants, bars, cafés and leisure venues across the UK. Everything we specify is built for the environment — upholstery to withstand commercial wear, frames rated for hospitality loading, finishes that resist spills and UV, and fire ratings documented for your file.

Whether it's a boutique hotel opening its first property, a restaurant group rolling out a new concept across multiple sites, or an established venue refurbishing a tired interior, we handle specification, supply, delivery and install as one joined-up project — keeping the trades in order and the opening date in sight.

How We Work

Brand-Right. Budget-Honest. Opening-Date Reliable.

Hospitality projects live on three deadlines: the opening night, the budget, and the brand. We build every project plan around all three.

For operators rolling out multiple sites we can hold a specified bedroom or dining pack and re-deploy it with minor adjustments across properties, keeping brand consistency without re-specifying from scratch each time. For one-off boutique venues we work from the creative brief — often alongside an interior designer or architect — translating mood boards into buildable furniture specifications, soft-quoted in days rather than weeks.

We handle the logistics realities hospitality projects always run into: scheduled deliveries into live venues, overnight installation to avoid trading disruption, storage before a staggered handover, and coordination with other trades on construction sites. The furniture is half the job — getting it in the door on the right day is the other half.
