# On-page SEO audit — switchcontractfurniture.co.uk

**Date:** 19 April 2026
**Scope:** 9 public HTML pages
**Overall grade:** B — solid technical foundation, but content depth and local signals are the ceilings holding rankings back.

---

## What's already good

The technical hygiene on this site is better than most small-business sites I audit. Keep these as they are.

Every page has a unique, descriptive title tag under 60 characters and a unique meta description in the 140–160 character sweet spot. Canonical tags are correct (all point to the `www` version, self-referential). Open Graph and Twitter Card metadata is complete on every page, so link previews on LinkedIn and Slack render properly. All 104 images across the site have alt text — a rare 100% score. LocalBusiness + FurnitureStore + WebSite schema is present on every page, with BreadcrumbList on most. Mobile viewport and favicon setup is all in place.

For B2B commercial intent pages Google typically wants 800–1,500+ words of genuinely useful content. Five of your nine pages are under 400 words. That's the biggest single thing holding rankings back.

## Critical issues (fix these first)

**1. Homepage H1 is weak as a ranking signal.**
The current H1 is "Your Partner For [rotating words]". The static text Google indexes is whatever word happens to be the default (currently "Task Chairs"). So your homepage H1 for Google says "Your Partner For Task Chairs" — clever visually, useless for SEO. A static, keyword-rich H1 like "UK Contract Furniture Supply, Planning & Installation" would rank meaningfully better for the terms you care about. The rotating-word effect can move to an H2 or a styled paragraph underneath.

**2. Sectors page is 3 landing pages crammed into 332 words.**
You're trying to rank for office furniture, education furniture, and hospitality furniture from one thin page. Google can't tell which query this page answers best. Split into three dedicated pages: `/office-furniture.html`, `/education-furniture.html`, `/hospitality-furniture.html`, each 800–1,200 words with their own sector-specific case studies, challenges, product types, and client types. This single change could triple your indexed surface area for high-intent queries.

**3. LocalBusiness schema is missing street address and postcode.**
Currently the schema only lists `"addressRegion": "Yorkshire"`. For local pack rankings and Google Business Profile alignment, you need full `streetAddress`, `postalCode`, and `addressLocality`. Also add `geo` coordinates (latitude/longitude) and populate the `sameAs` array with your LinkedIn, Instagram, and any other social profiles.

**4. Services, brands, and about pages are thin on content.**
At 337–381 words each, these pages give Google nothing to grab. Target expansion: services page should detail each service (supply, space planning, procurement, delivery, installation, aftercare) with process, timescales, and typical project size. Brands page should let you target brand-specific long-tail queries ("Boss Design furniture UK", "Humanscale chair supplier") — add 2–3 sentences per named manufacturer. About page needs founder/team bios, company history, trade memberships, and named client references for E-E-A-T.

## Medium-priority issues

**5. Missing BreadcrumbList schema on 3 pages.**
Sectors, brands, and inspiration don't have breadcrumb schema. Trivial to add — copy the pattern from services.html.

**6. No FAQ schema anywhere.**
FAQ schema is the single biggest rich-result opportunity for B2B service sites. Add 4–6 FAQs per major page (services, sectors, contact) with `FAQPage` schema. Questions like "What is contract-grade furniture?", "How long does office furniture installation take?", "Do you deliver across the UK?" can earn you expandable FAQ boxes directly in Google SERPs.

**7. Contact page is 201 words with 1 H2.**
Add an FAQ section ("What happens after I submit a quote?", "How quickly will you respond?", "Do you charge for site visits?"), service areas ("Serving offices across Leeds, Manchester, London..."), and response-time commitments. This also helps conversion, not just SEO.

**8. No case studies / proof pages.**
You have logos from Triage, YorLMC, Market Harborough Building Society, Stauff, Fornax, Hawkesworth, Integrated Skills, Octric, and The Group Company in the carousel but no written case studies. Every finished project is a ranking asset: title it "[Client] Office Furniture Refit — [City]", 600–1,000 words with problem/solution/outcome, and you get a page that ranks for client name + competitor research queries AND proves expertise for Google's E-E-A-T signals.

**9. Internal linking is shallow.**
From each page I see navigation links but few contextual in-body links between related content. When services.html talks about hospitality fit-out, link those words to the (future) hospitality sector page. Google follows internal anchor text to understand topic relationships.

**10. Inspiration page is 44 images / 317 words.**
Image-rich pages rank poorly without supporting text. Add category descriptions ("Task chairs for modern offices — what to look for"), filter/sort intro copy, and brief context blurbs between image groups. Also worth adding `ImageObject` schema for key product images.

## Low-priority nits

- The `<meta name="keywords">` tag is ignored by Google and carries trivial weight with Bing. Harmless but you can remove it if tidying.
- `og:image` on the homepage points to `logo.png`. Social share previews would look sharper pointing to `hero-stauff.jpg` (which you already use in schema `image`).
- Consider `hreflang` tags if you ever publish UK/US/EU variants (not urgent).
- No `robots` meta tag anywhere — fine, default is `index, follow`, but worth adding explicitly to private pages if any exist.
- Homepage `FurnitureStore` schema may get you misclassified as a retail store by Google — consider dropping and using just `LocalBusiness` + `ProfessionalService` to position as a B2B supplier rather than consumer retail.
